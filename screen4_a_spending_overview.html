Health Savings Account
HSA
What it is
A tax-advantaged account for medical expenses. Money you contribute is pre-tax, the balance grows tax-free, and withdrawals for qualified medical expenses are tax-free too — the only "triple tax-advantage" account in the U.S. Funds roll over year to year (unlike FSA), and you can invest the balance like a 401(k).
Why it doesn't apply to you
HSAs are only available with an HDHP medical plan — that's an IRS rule, not Acme's. You picked the PPO this year (the closest match to what you described), so you're not HSA-eligible. If you switch to HDHP next enrollment, this opens up.

Limited Purpose FSA
LPFSA
What it is
A special version of FSA that only covers dental and vision expenses — not medical. The "Limited Purpose" exists specifically so it can be used alongside an HSA without disqualifying HSA contributions.
Why it doesn't apply to you
LPFSA is designed to pair with HSA. Since you don't have an HSA this year, the regular FSA above already covers everything LPFSA would, plus medical expenses. There's no scenario where LPFSA would be better for you than the FSA you've already got.
