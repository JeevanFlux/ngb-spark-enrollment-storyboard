💡 What's supplemental insurance, exactly?

Your medical insurance pays the hospital and doctors when you use care. Supplemental insurance is different — it pays you directly, in cash, when something specific happens (an accident, a hospital stay).

That cash isn't earmarked for medical bills. It's yours to use however you need — your deductible, your copays, time off work, childcare during a hospital stay, food, transport, anything.

Medical insurance Pays the hospital. Covers your medical bills directly. Already in your file: PPO with $4,500 family OOP max.
Supplemental insurance Pays you cash. Covers everything else — lost wages, family transport, the deductible itself, etc.

Wexley's read for your situation

Hospital indemnity is most valuable for people on HDHP medical plans (high deductible, big out-of-pocket exposure) or households with no other safety net. You're on PPO with a $4,500 family OOP max — solid coverage. So the financial argument here is weaker than it would be for someone on HDHP.

That said: hospital stays bring non-medical costs (lost wages, childcare for Maya during a stay, transport, food). Some PPO families still keep this for peace of mind; others skip it as duplicate protection. Either is defensible. Up to you.
