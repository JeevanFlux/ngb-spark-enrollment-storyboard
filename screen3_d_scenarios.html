What this means for your decision

The PPO costs $1,365/year more in paycheck deductions, but saves you up to $2,700 if your year goes badly.

Net-net: even one rough event in the year — Maya getting sick, anyone needing an MRI — and the PPO is already cheaper. That's why it's the closest match for your situation. The HDHP only saves money if your whole year stays healthy and you max your HSA contributions.

You're a 28-year-old with a 2-year-old, daily meds, and a husband. The "all-healthy year" scenario is real but the math leans toward the PPO trade-off for your specific inputs. Both plans are defensible options — your call which trade-off fits your tolerance.
