Wexley's read · what the trade-off looks like for your situation
The HDHP saves you ~$400 in a healthy year — that's real money. But it costs ~$2,700 more in a rough year, and with daily meds and a 2-year-old, the math leans toward predictable usage rather than healthy. You said you wanted fewer surprises, and the PPO's $4,500 worst-case cap (vs. $7,500 on the HDHP) is what that looks like in numbers.

Both plans are defensible. The HDHP makes sense if you're confident in a healthy year and want the HSA tax savings. This is your call — I'm here to lay out the trade-offs, not to make the decision for you.
